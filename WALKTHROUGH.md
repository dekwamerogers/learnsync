## Section 1 — The Daily Briefing (Home Page)

**Time: ~5 minutes**

> *"This is the home page — the daily briefing. Every morning, you open this page to understand the state of the entire learner portfolio at a glance."*

### 1.1 Freshness bar (top of page)
Point to the bar at the top.
> *"The system always tells you when the data was last refreshed and which file was used. If it says 'upload overdue', data is more than 7 days old and you need to re-upload."*

### 1.2 Action row — Follow-ups and New Learners

Point to the two cards at the top of the page.

- **Follow-ups Due** — orange card. *"These are learners whose intervention was logged and we said we'd follow up today. Clicking this takes us straight to that filtered list."*
- **New This Week** — blue card. *"Learners enrolled in the last 7 days. Good for checking onboarding velocity."*

> **Demo tip:** Click "Follow-ups Due" to show the filtered learner list, then come back to Home.

### 1.3 Health strip

Five tiles in a row (Dormant, At Risk, Active, Graduated, Not Started).

> *"These are learner-level health counts — one person counted once regardless of how many programmes they're in. The colour-coded labels tell you the distribution at a glance. The active-wins rule applies: if a learner is active in one programme and dormant in another, they count as Active overall."*

- Click **Dormant** to show dormant learners list. Note the filters auto-apply.
- Come back and click **At Risk**.

> *"From any of these tiles you can drill straight into the exact learner segment."*

### 1.4 Portfolio KPIs — Unique Learners & Enrolments

Point to the merged tile.

> *"Unique Learners is the count of distinct people in the eHub activity export — these are learners actively using the platform. Below that is the roster-only number: learners we've enrolled via the admin roster upload who haven't appeared in eHub yet — they're coming but haven't started."*

> *"Enrolments is a different count: one learner in three programmes = 3 enrolments. The breakdown below shows how those enrolments are distributed by health status."*

### 1.5 Learner Journey funnel

> *"This funnel tracks the full progression pipeline:*
> - *Onboarded = completed WALX (the prerequisite)*
> - *Onboarding = currently in WALX but not graduated*
> - *Activated = passed Module 1 of their main programme*
> - *Retained = passed Module 1 AND still Active or At Risk — i.e. they kept going*"

*"The percentages show conversion at each stage. Low retention rate = learners are passing Module 1 but then stalling."*

### 1.6 Charts

Point to the two chart cards.

> *"The donut shows the portfolio health breakdown visually — one glance, you know the shape of your cohort.*
> *The grouped bar breaks that down by programme — useful to see which programmes are healthy and which are struggling."*

**Fullscreen demo:**
> *"All charts across the platform have a fullscreen button in the top-right corner. Click it to expand for a presentation."*

Click the ⛶ icon on the grouped bar chart. Press Esc to exit.

### 1.7 Flag Breakdown

Point to the "At-Risk & Dormant — Flag Breakdown" card.

> *"This horizontal bar chart shows exactly WHY learners are at risk or dormant. The flags are:*
> - *Inactive — no recent course activity*
> - *Never Activated — enrolled but never passed a course*
> - *Stuck on Assignment — failing the same assignment repeatedly*
> - *Low Pass Rate — struggling consistently across courses*
> - *Stalled Between Courses — gap too long between completions*
> - *Stalled Progression — no forward movement*
> - *Payment Issue — overdue payment flag*"

*"Clicking a bar filters the learner list to everyone carrying that flag."*

### 1.8 Programme Health Breakdown table

Scroll down to the table.

> *"This table gives you a per-programme snapshot. In eHub is the active count in the platform. Roster Only is people enrolled but not yet in eHub — useful to spot programmes where we've signed people up but onboarding hasn't started.*
> *Click any coloured number — Active, At Risk, Dormant, Graduated — to jump to that filtered learner list."*

### 1.9 Enrolments by Week chart

Scroll down to the stacked bar timeline.

> *"This chart shows when learners enrolled by week, broken down by programme. It lets you see onboarding waves — useful for identifying spikes and capacity planning."*

## Section 7 — Uploading Data

**Time: ~2 minutes**

> *"The system is data-driven — everything you see depends on two types of CSV:*
> 1. **Activity CSV** (from eHub staff portal) — records all learner activity: course completions, attempts, scores. This is the primary data source.*
> 2. **Enrolment/Roster CSV** (from your own records) — adds learners who are enrolled but not yet in eHub.*"

*Click **Upload** (the button in the top-right or sidebar). The upload form has two fields: the CSV file and a 'Data as of date' (when the file was exported from the staff portal — fill this in, it prevents learners from appearing artificially dormant).*

*Once you click Upload & Queue, a real-time progress bar shows the file uploading to the server. When the upload finishes, the page automatically moves to an **Analysis** screen — you'll see three steps ticking off live: Reading & parsing CSV → Detecting programmes & courses → Counting new learners. This usually takes 10–30 seconds for large files.*

*When analysis completes you land on a **Review** screen. This shows a breakdown by programme (rows found, new courses detected, any unrecognised rows flagged). Review it, then click Confirm to run the full ingestion — or Cancel to discard.*

*After you confirm, a 7-step pipeline runs in the background with its own progress bar. The ingestion log (Admin → Ingestion Log) shows completed, in-progress, and failed jobs.*

*Best practice: upload the enrolment CSV first, then the activity CSV. They can run simultaneously without conflict, but enrolment-only learners get their records created first this way.*

## Questions & Common Scenarios

### "How do I find learners who enrolled but never started?"
> Filter learner list by **Health: Not Started** (or use the Programme Health Breakdown table on the home page → Not Started column).

### "What does 'Roster Only' mean on the home page?"
> These learners appear in your enrolment upload (you've signed them up) but have never appeared in eHub activity data. They're enrolled but not yet active on the platform.

### "Why does the graduation rate look lower than I expected?"
> Graduation rate = graduates ÷ module-activated learners (those who passed at least Module 1). Learners who enrolled but never passed Module 1 don't count in the denominator — the metric only measures conversion within the active cohort.

### "How often should I upload data?"
> Ideally daily or at least twice a week. The freshness indicator at the top turns amber (and the home page shows "upload overdue") if data is more than 7 days old.

### "Can I export the learner list?"
> Yes — the learner list has an Export CSV button in the top-right. It exports all currently filtered results including health status, flags, enrolment dates, and intervention history.
